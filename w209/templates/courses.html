Impact of Enrollment Size on Academic Performance by Region

The visual below depicts the connection between school size and academic performance.The interactive plots juxtapose 4th and 8th grade math and reading scores against total enrollments in respective grades.The observed patterns from the visual indicate that smaller enrollment sizes may have correlaton with higher performance scores. Using this visual you can select a region through the checkboxes or highlight the color coded regions from the legends and find out the connection between student total enrollment in that specific region and the scores.The size of the bubble in the scatter plot is proportional to the enrollment count. The plot also includes a year filter dropdown, allowing users to select and view data for a specific year.



Instruction Expenditure and Performance Trends in the U.S

The visual depicts a geographical map highlighting all U.S. states, complemented by two line graphs below.This is an interactive US map. Once a state is clicked, two line graphs appear below: one detailing expenditure per student and the other showing the trend of average math and reading scores for grades 4 and 8.



Regional Academic Performance in Math and Reading: Grade 4 vs. Grade 8

The interactive plot features stacked bar charts with color-coded categories for above average, average, and below average scores. Clicking on the bars brings up a detailed table showing state, average scores for reading, math and enrollment counts for the corresponding region,score category and grades.All regions demonstrate a mix of proficiency levels, with no single region consistently outperforming the others across all categories.



Grade 4 and 8 Score Leaders by State

The tables display the highest achieving states in Grade 4 and Grade 8 math and reading scores from 2003 to 2015 with Massachusetts consistently leading in both subjects and grades, with the highest scores almost every year.

Conclusion

The analysis suggests that smaller school enrollments could result in improved academic outcomes for 4th and 8th graders in the U.S. Despite rising instructional expenditure per student from 2004 to 2015, academic performance in math and reading has not shown significant improvement. A regional analysis reveals varied levels of proficiency across the Midwest, Northeast, South, and West, with no region consistently outperforming others.Massachusetts stands out for its consistent high performance in math and reading for both 4th and 8th grades from 2003 to 2015.
