Tracing the Evolution of U.S. Educational Spending

Educational Spending Trends by State and Year

The horizontal bar chart below ranks states by average instructional expenditure per student, showing variability across states, with the highest spending state at the top and the lowest on the bottom. Following this, you will see a line graph which depicts each state’s spending over time, starting with a view of the average spending for all states. Each state shows a steady increase over time





Instructional Spending Over Time

The dashboard below shows two slope graphs that represents two views. On the left, the average instructional expenditure per student per region is depicted. Using this visual you can select a region which will highlight all the corresponding states on the right hand side. The slope graph on the right shows each individual state’s spending trajectory from 1994 to 2015. All states and regions show an increase over time with some of the highest spending states like New York showing a steep incline. The color coding distinguishes between regions, aiding in regional comparison of spending growth



Trends in Instruction Expenditure per Student and Enrollment Over Time

The visualization comprises of two area charts that compare educational metrics across various states from 1993 to 2016. It allows users to analyze trends in instructional spending per student and student enrollment, highlighting patterns like the general rise in spending and changes in enrollment.States such as the District of Columbia have seen fluctuating trends in instructional expenditure per student and enrollment. Meanwhile, states like Michigan and Maine have experienced a decrease in enrollment trends, while the instructional expenditure per student in these states has shown an overall increase. This format aids in understanding the unique educational investments and performances of each state.



Inflation and population

Instructional spending per student has increased over time as has enrollment. However, to understand the big picture we must also look at this in context. The rise in instructional spending is not only due to a rise in enrollment, but rather a rise in inflation, and a rise in the overall population in the United States. We use the Consumer Price Index as an indicator of inflation in the chart below as it shows how prices for goods and services have changed over time



Dynamic Educational Expenditure Rankings by State

This animated chart captures the changing ranks of the top 20 U.S. states in terms of instructional expenditure over time. It highlights the fluctuations in education spending across different states with a dropdown menu to select specific years for closer inspection. California and New York consistently appear at the top, reflecting their large educational budgets.

Conclusion

Overall, instructional spending per student and total spending per student have increased over time. This has been due to a rise in enrollment which is connected to both a rise in population and a rise in inflation. States have increased their spending steadily in response to these factors. In parallel, regional spending has also increased though some states in each region stand out as having higher enrollment and spending totals.
